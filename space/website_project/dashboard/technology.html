NOAA's Solar Activity Data API (GOES Satellite):

The GOES satellite monitors the 10.7 cm solar radio flux, a key measure of solar activity. This flux is associated with emissions from the sun’s outer atmosphere and helps in understanding solar energy output variations.

Our Technology

Data Collection

We gather real-time solar activity data from two primary sources:

  • NOAA's Solar Activity Data API: Provides real-time measurements of solar flux, a key indicator of solar activity.
  • NASA's HelioViewer API: Accesses high-quality solar images and related metadata, enriching our dataset.

Machine Learning

Our model leverages LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) networks, a specialized type of Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) designed to handle time-series data. LSTMs are well suited for predicting future solar activity based on historical patterns.

Model Training

We ensure the model performs effectively through a rigorous data preprocessing pipeline:

  1. Standardize solar flux data for consistency.
  2. Segment the time-series data into sequences.
  3. Train the model using past solar flux data, enabling it to learn flux variations over time.
